# Home Ratification Guide

Before running the detector, perform a **ratification** — an ultrasonic survey of your home to identify and eliminate non-rat sources of ultrasonic noise. This improves the quality of life for your rats and ensures detection accuracy.

## Equipment needed

- **Ultrasonic microphone** (e.g., Dodotronic Ultramic SO.104)
- **XLR cable**
- **Audio interface** (e.g., Motu M2)
- **Laptop with a DAW or spectrum analyzer** (e.g., REAPER, Voxengo SPAN)
- **Cardboard poster tube** (optional, for isolating noise sources)

## Common ultrasonic noise sources

| Source | Frequency Range | Why it emits |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| CFL bulbs | 20–60 kHz | Electronic ballasts use switching power converters |
| Low-quality LED bulbs | 15–40 kHz | Poor drivers cause electromagnetic interference |
| LED strip lights | 15–60 kHz | Magnetic transformers and low-quality drivers |
| AC/DC chargers ("wall warts") | 20–80 kHz | Transformer/switching power supply interference |
| Desktop/laptop power supplies | 20–100 kHz | High-frequency switching in power conversion |
| Motion detectors/security sensors | 20+ kHz | Designed to emit ultrasound |
| Old CRT televisions | 15–40 kHz | Internal electronics emit high-pitched sounds |

## Ratification procedure

1. **Walk your home** with the ultrasonic microphone connected to the audio interface and visualized in a spectrum analyzer
2. **Identify loud sources** — focus on energy above 18 kHz; compare different rooms and areas
3. **Isolate sources** — use the cardboard tube as a directional probe to focus on individual devices
4. **Eliminate or move** sources using the mitigation strategies below (most effective first)

## Mitigation strategies

### Replace the noise source (most effective)

- **Swap low-quality chargers/adapters** for higher-quality ones from reputable brands (Apple, Anker, Dell, etc.)
- **Replace CFL bulbs** with LED bulbs; replace buzzing LEDs with high-quality alternatives
- **Replace surge protectors with USB ports** using models without USB or from better manufacturers
- **Contact device manufacturers** (modem, router, etc.) for replacement units if transformers are defective

### Physical isolation & dampening

- **Move noisy devices** into closets, under furniture, or behind heavy objects where fabric absorbs ultrasound
- **Use vibration dampening** — place adapters/devices on rubber pads, felt, or cork to reduce vibration transmission
- **Enclose small devices** in a box or eyeglass case with foam (ensure adequate ventilation to prevent overheating)
- **Add acoustic dampening** with area rugs, curtains, or bulletin boards

### Electrical fixes

- **Try different outlets** — plug devices directly into wall outlets (bypass surge protectors/UPS) to test if the power path is the issue
- **Tighten wall socket connections** — loose connections can arc and cause buzzing
- **For fluorescent lights**, replace bad bulbs or magnetic ballasts with electronic ballasts

### Masking (if elimination is not possible)

- **Run a fan** to mask high-frequency whines
- **Use a white noise machine** to dull noticeable ultrasonic pitches

## Goal

A clean ultrasonic baseline where most energy above 20 kHz comes from the rats, not the environment. Once ratified, you can confidently run Pinnae knowing that detections are genuine rat vocalizations.
